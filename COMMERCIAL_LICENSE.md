If your use case doesn't allow open-sourcing your code, a commercial license removes the AGPL obligations.

## When you need a commercial license

- You want to embed reframe into a **closed-source SaaS product** (design tools, creative automation, ad platforms, etc.)
- You want to integrate reframe into **proprietary software** without releasing your source code
- You want to offer reframe as part of a **managed service** to your customers
- Your legal team requires a non-copyleft license for internal use

## When you don't need a commercial license

- You're using reframe for **personal or internal projects** and are fine with AGPL terms
- You're building an **open source project** that complies with AGPL-3.0
- You're using reframe for **research, education, or evaluation**
